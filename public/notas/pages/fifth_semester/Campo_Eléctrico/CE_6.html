Aprovechando que tenemos la distribución lineal de la carga del anillo, podemos usarla como diferencial de carga del disco. Así: \(dQ = q\)
Entonces: \[ \|\vec{dE}\| = \frac{qz}{4\pi \varepsilon_0 \left(r^2 + z^2\right)^{3/2}} \] \[ \|\vec{dE}\| = \frac{dQz}{4\pi \varepsilon_0 \left(r^2 + z^2\right)^{3/2}} \] Usando la densidad de carga superficial: \[ \frac{dQ}{dA}, \quad dQ = \sigma \, dA \] \[ \|\vec{dE}\| = \frac{z \sigma \, dA}{4\pi \varepsilon_0 \left(r^2 + z^2\right)^{3/2}} \] \[ \|\vec{E}\| = \int_0^R \frac{z \sigma \, dA}{4\pi \varepsilon_0 \left(r^2 + z^2\right)^{3/2}} \] \[ dA = 2\pi r \, dr \] \[ \|\vec{E}\| = \int_0^R \frac{z \sigma 2\pi r \, dr}{4\pi \varepsilon_0 \left(r^2 + z^2\right)^{3/2}} \] Resolviendo por cambio de variable: \[ u = r^2 + z^2 \] \[ du = 2rdr \] \[ \|\vec{E}\| = \int_0^R \frac{z \sigma 2\pi r \left(\frac{du}{2r}\right)}{4\pi \varepsilon_0 u^{3/2}} \] \[ \|\vec{E}\| = \int_0^R \frac{z \sigma \pi \, du}{4\pi \varepsilon_0 u^{3/2}} \] \[ \|\vec{E}\| = \frac{z \sigma \pi}{4\pi \varepsilon_0} \left[ -\frac{u^{-1/2}}{1/2} \right]_0^R \] \[ \|\vec{E}\| = \frac{-z \sigma \pi}{2\pi \varepsilon_0} \left[ \left(r^2 + z^2 \right)^{-1/2} \right]_0^R \] \[ \|\vec{E}\| = \frac{-z \sigma \pi}{2\pi \varepsilon_0} \left\{ \left(R^2 + z^2 \right)^{-1/2} - \left(0 + z^2 \right)^{-1/2} \right\} \] \[ \|\vec{E}\| = \frac{z \sigma \pi}{2\pi \varepsilon_0} \left\{ \left(z^2 \right)^{-1/2} - \left(R^2 + z^2 \right)^{-1/2} \right\} \] \[ \|\vec{E}\| = \frac{z \sigma \pi}{2\pi \varepsilon_0} \left\{ \frac{1}{z} - \frac{1}{\left(R^2 + z^2 \right)^{1/2}} \right\} \quad \text{resultado válido.} \] \[ \|\vec{E}\| = \frac{\sigma}{2 \varepsilon_0} \left\{ \frac{z}{z} - \frac{z}{\left(R^2 + z^2 \right)^{1/2}} \right\} \] \[ \|\vec{E}\| = \frac{\sigma}{2 \varepsilon_0} \left\{ 1 - \frac{z}{\left(R^2 + z^2 \right)^{1/2}} \right\} \quad \text{resultado válido.} \] Si multiplicamos numerador y denominador por \(\pi R^2\): \[ \|\vec{E}\| = \frac{\pi R^2 \sigma}{2 \pi R^2 \varepsilon_0} \left\{ 1 - \frac{z}{\left(R^2 + z^2 \right)^{1/2}} \right\} \]

\[\|\vec{E}\| = \frac{Q}{2 \pi R^2 \varepsilon_0} \left\{ 1 - \frac{z}{\left( R^2 + z^2 \right)^{1/2}} \right\} \]

Resultado del libro.

Ahora, consideremos un disco de radio \(R\) uniformemente cargado, donde \(R>> Z\). (lámina infinita) De uno de los resultados obtenidos: \[ \|\vec{E}\| = \frac{\sigma}{2 \varepsilon_0} \left\{ 1 - \frac{z}{\left( R^2 + z^2 \right)^{1/2}} \right\} \] Nos queda:

\[ \|\vec{E}\| = \frac{\sigma}{2 \varepsilon_0} \]
